Introduction About This Dataset¶

This data set contains information about "10,000" movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. Dataset link

dataset has the following features:-

  • imdb_id - A unique identifier for each movie.
  • cast - The name of lead and supporting actors.
    • director - The name of Director
    • budget - The budget in which the movie was made.
    • budget_adj - show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars,
    • genres - The genre of the movie, Action, Comedy ,Thriller etc.
    • homepage - A link to the homepage of the movie.
    • keywords - The keywords or tags related to the movie.
    • original_title - The title of the movie before translation or adaptation.
    • overview - A brief description of the movie.
    • popularity - A numeric quantity specifying the movie popularity.
    • production_companies - The production house of the movie.
    • release_date - The date on which it was released.
    • release_year - The year on which it was released.
    • revenue - The worldwide revenue generated by the movie.
    • revenue_adj-show the revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars
    • runtime - The running time of the movie in minutes.
    • tagline - Movie's tagline.
    • vote_average - average ratings the movie recieved.
    • vote_count - the count of votes recieved

    </ul>

Questions that will be answered:¶

  • Which movie have high popularity over this years ?
  • Is there a relation between budget and revenue ?
  • What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have high revenues ?
  • Which Genres have high popularity in specific year or period time ?

def high_movie_popularity(year1 = 0 , year2 = 0) :
    ''' this function will give us the most popular Movies 
        Parameters :
        year1 : int b
               if you not give any number the function will give you insights of all years
               if you give a specific year the function will give you insights of this years
        year2 : int (year between year_1 to 2016)
                if you give a specific year the function will give you insights between year_1 and year_2
    '''
    sorted_popularity = df.sort_values(by ='popularity',ascending= False)
    if year2 == 0 :
        year2 = year1
    if year1 != 0 :
        sorted_popularity.query('release_year >= @year1 and release_year <= @year2',inplace = True)
        
    plt.subplots(figsize=(10,8))
    plt.gca().invert_yaxis()
    plt.barh(sorted_popularity['original_title'].head(10),sorted_popularity['popularity'].head(10))
